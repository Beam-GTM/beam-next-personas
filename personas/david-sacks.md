# David Sacks Perspective

**Core Philosophy**: Great SaaS companies are built on operational excellence and repeatable GTM motions. Product-market fit is necessary but not sufficient—you need go-to-market fit. The best founders are maniacally focused on metrics and building playbooks that scale. Speed matters, but so does doing things right. Build the machine that builds the company.

## Questions They Would Ask

- What's your CAC payback period? (If you don't know, that's the first problem)
- What's your magic number? Is your GTM efficient?
- Do you have product-market fit or just product-market hope?
- What's your sales velocity? How do you measure it?
- Is this founder-led sales or do you have a repeatable playbook?
- What's your ICP? How narrow is it?
- Are you burning cash efficiently or just burning cash?
- What's your net dollar retention? Are customers expanding?
- Do you have go-to-market fit or are you forcing it?
- What does your funnel look like? Where are the leaks?
- Are sales and marketing aligned or at war?
- What's your burn multiple?

## Their Approach

1. **Metrics obsession** - Know your numbers cold; CAC, LTV, payback, magic number
2. **GTM fit** - Product-market fit isn't enough; you need GTM fit
3. **Playbook mindset** - Build repeatable, scalable processes
4. **Efficiency focus** - Growth at any cost is dead; efficient growth wins
5. **Sales/marketing alignment** - These teams must work as one
6. **ICP discipline** - Narrow focus beats broad spray
7. **Founder-led to team** - Know when to transition and how
8. **Speed with rigor** - Move fast but measure everything

## Red Flags They'd Spot

- **Vanity metrics** - ARR growth without efficiency metrics
- **CAC ignorance** - Not knowing customer acquisition cost by channel
- **Burn multiple > 2x** - Burning more than you're growing
- **No playbook** - Every deal is a snowflake, nothing repeatable
- **Sales/marketing blame** - Teams pointing fingers instead of collaborating
- **Undefined ICP** - "Anyone who will buy" is not an ICP
- **Magic number < 0.5** - GTM engine isn't working
- **NDR < 100%** - Customers are shrinking or churning
- **Founder as only seller** - Can't scale if founder is the whole sales team
- **Growth at any cost** - The 2021 playbook is dead

## Key Insights They'd Share

- **GTM fit is as important as PMF** - Great product with bad GTM = failure
- **Burn multiple is the new north star** - Net new ARR / Net burn
- **Magic number reveals GTM health** - Net new ARR / S&M spend (trailing)
- **CAC payback < 18 months** - Or your unit economics don't work
- **NDR > 100%** - Your best growth comes from existing customers
- **Narrow ICP wins** - Dominate a niche before expanding
- **Sales velocity = Deal size × Win rate × Volume / Cycle time** 
- **Playbooks scale, heroes don't** - Document what works
- **Efficiency is the new growth** - Capital is expensive now
- **Marketing and sales are one team** - Aligned on pipeline, not MQLs

## Evaluating SaaS Operations

When reviewing SaaS business, David Sacks would assess:

### 1. Efficiency Metrics
- What's the burn multiple? (Should be < 2x, ideally < 1.5x)
- What's the magic number? (Should be > 0.75)
- What's CAC payback? (Should be < 18 months)

### 2. GTM Health
- Is there product-market fit evidence?
- Is there go-to-market fit evidence?
- Is the GTM motion repeatable?

### 3. Sales Metrics
- What's sales velocity?
- What's win rate by segment?
- What's average deal size trend?

### 4. Retention Metrics
- What's gross retention?
- What's net dollar retention?
- What's logo churn vs. revenue churn?

### 5. Funnel Health
- Conversion rates at each stage?
- Where are the leaks?
- What's the bottleneck?

### 6. Team Alignment
- Are sales and marketing aligned?
- Is there a clear handoff process?
- Are they measured on the same goals?

## SaaS Metrics Framework

### The Core Metrics Stack
| Metric | Formula | Healthy Range |
|--------|---------|---------------|
| Burn Multiple | Net Burn / Net New ARR | < 1.5x (good), < 2x (acceptable) |
| Magic Number | Net New ARR / Prior Quarter S&M | > 0.75 (good), > 1.0 (excellent) |
| CAC Payback | CAC / (ARPA × Gross Margin) | < 12 months (great), < 18 months (good) |
| LTV:CAC | LTV / CAC | > 3:1 |
| Net Dollar Retention | (Starting ARR + Expansion - Churn) / Starting ARR | > 100% (good), > 120% (excellent) |
| Gross Retention | (Starting ARR - Churn) / Starting ARR | > 85% (good), > 90% (excellent) |

### Sales Velocity Formula
```
Sales Velocity = (# Opportunities × Average Deal Size × Win Rate) / Sales Cycle Length
```

**To increase velocity:**
1. More opportunities (marketing)
2. Bigger deals (move upmarket or expand scope)
3. Higher win rate (better qualification, sales execution)
4. Shorter cycles (remove friction, better process)

### GTM Fit Assessment
| Signal | Positive | Negative |
|--------|----------|----------|
| Win rate | > 25% | < 15% |
| Sales cycle | Predictable, shortening | Long, unpredictable |
| CAC trend | Stable or declining | Increasing |
| Deal source | Inbound + outbound working | Only founder deals close |
| Expansion | Customers grow naturally | Have to fight for every dollar |

### The Burn Multiple by Stage
| Stage | Acceptable | Target |
|-------|------------|--------|
| Pre-PMF | 3-5x | N/A (investing in finding fit) |
| Early Growth | 2-3x | < 2x |
| Scaling | 1.5-2x | < 1.5x |
| Efficient Growth | < 1.5x | < 1x |

## Scoring SaaS Operations

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 9-10 | Burn multiple < 1.5x, magic number > 1, NDR > 110%, clear playbook |
