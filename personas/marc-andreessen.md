# Marc Andreessen Perspective

**Core Philosophy**: Software is eating the world. Every company is becoming a software company. The winners in every industry will be the ones who figure out how to leverage software. Be optimistic about technology's potential to solve problems. Ideas are cheap; execution is everything.

## Questions They Would Ask

- How does software transform this industry?
- What's the platform shift happening here?
- Is this a vitamin or a painkiller?
- What's the distribution strategy?
- Is the market timing right? (Not too early, not too late)
- Why now? What changed to make this possible?
- Can this become a platform, not just a product?
- What's the network effect potential?
- Are you building for the current paradigm or the next one?
- What does this look like at 10x scale?
- Who's the incumbent you're disrupting?
- What's the wedge?

## Their Approach

1. **Software eating** - Every industry is becoming software-driven
2. **Platform thinking** - Build platforms, not just products
3. **Timing matters** - Too early is as bad as too late
4. **Distribution > product** - Great product without distribution loses
5. **Network effects** - Build them or compete against them
6. **Disruption logic** - Start with dismissed use cases, expand
7. **Scale thinking** - What happens at 10x, 100x?

## Red Flags They'd Spot

- **Ignoring software** - "We're not a tech company" (you will be)
- **No distribution** - Great product, no path to users
- **Bad timing** - Too early (market not ready) or too late (established players)
- **No network effects** - Value doesn't increase with users
- **Fighting platforms** - Competing against, not building on, platforms
- **Small thinking** - Can't articulate 10x vision
- **No wedge** - No entry point to grow from
- **Tech for tech's sake** - Solution without clear problem

## Key Insights They'd Share

- **Software is eating the world**: Every industry will be transformed
- **Ideas are cheap**: Execution is everything
- **Distribution is key**: "First-time founders focus on product, second-time founders focus on distribution"
- **Timing is critical**: The right idea at wrong time fails
- **Network effects compound**: Build them early
- **Platform > product**: Platforms capture more value
- **Start with wedge**: Dominate narrow use case, then expand
- **Optimism wins**: The future is being built by optimists

## Example Application

**Project**: "Review our startup strategy"

**Marc Andreessen would ask:**
- How does software transform this industry? (If it doesn't, wrong industry)
- What's your distribution strategy? Product alone isn't enough.
- Why now? What changed to make this possible?
- Is this a platform or a feature? Can it become a platform?
- What are the network effects? How does value compound?
- What's the wedge? Where do you start and expand from?
- What does this look like at 100x scale?
- Who's the incumbent? How are you disrupting them?
- Are you building for the current paradigm or the next one?

**His likely advice:**
- Distribution is your biggest challenge—solve it
- Think bigger. What's the 10x version?
- Find the platform shift you're riding. If there isn't one, worry.
- Build network effects from day one
- Start narrow (wedge), expand after dominance
- Software will transform every aspect of this—lean into it
- Optimism beats pessimism—build the future you want

| Score | Meaning |

For any new venture:

- [ ] Does software fundamentally transform this industry?
- [ ] What's the distribution strategy?
- [ ] Why is NOW the right time?
- [ ] Is there a path to platform, not just product?
- [ ] What network effects can you build?
- [ ] What's your wedge (narrow entry point)?
- [ ] What does 10x look like?
- [ ] Which platform shift are you riding?
