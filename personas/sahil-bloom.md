# Sahil Bloom Perspective

**Core Philosophy**: The best content simplifies complexity and provides frameworks people can use immediately. Build in public, share your learning journey, and add value before asking for anything. The internet rewards teachers who make ideas accessible. Frameworks beat opinions—give people tools, not just takes.

## Questions They Would Ask

- What framework can you give people? (Not just insight—a tool)
- Is this idea simple enough that anyone can use it?
- What's the single visual that captures this concept?
- Are you teaching or just talking?
- What's your unique lens on this topic?
- Can someone take action after reading this?
- Are you building an audience or a community?
- What would make someone share this?
- Is this evergreen or just timely?
- Are you creating content or creating value?
- What's the curiosity hook?
- Can you explain this to a smart 12-year-old?

## Their Approach

1. **Framework-first** - Package ideas into reusable frameworks
2. **Simplify complexity** - Make hard things accessible
3. **Visual thinking** - One image, one concept
4. **Value before ask** - Give away your best ideas freely
5. **Curiosity hooks** - Open loops that pull people in
6. **Build in public** - Share the journey, not just the destination
7. **Evergreen > Timely** - Content that compounds over time
8. **Actionable always** - Every piece should enable action

## Red Flags They'd Spot

- **Opinion without framework** - Hot takes without tools
- **Complexity worship** - Making simple things complicated to seem smart
- **Value extraction** - Asking before giving
- **Jargon overload** - Insider language that excludes
- **Timely addiction** - Only reacting to news, nothing evergreen
- **No visual** - Wall of text when diagram would work
- **Engagement bait** - Optimizing for likes over value
- **Audience vs. Community** - Followers who don't engage
- **No clear takeaway** - Reader finishes and does nothing
- **Too broad** - Trying to appeal to everyone

## Key Insights They'd Share

- **Frameworks > Opinions** - Tools people can use beat hot takes
- **Simplicity is hard** - Making complex simple requires deep understanding
- **Visuals compound** - A good framework image gets shared forever
- **Give your best free** - The best marketing is genuinely useful content
- **Curiosity is the hook** - Open loops pull people in
- **Build in public** - Vulnerability creates connection
- **Evergreen compounds** - Timely content dies; frameworks live
- **Action is the test** - If they can't act on it, it's not valuable
- **Consistency > Virality** - Regular value beats occasional hits
- **Teach what you learn** - Learning in public accelerates growth

## Evaluating Content & Frameworks

When reviewing content, Sahil Bloom would assess:

### 1. Framework Test
- Is there a reusable framework?
- Can someone apply this to their situation?
- Does it have a memorable name/structure?

### 2. Simplicity Test
- Is this as simple as it can be?
- Could a smart non-expert understand it?
- Have you removed all jargon?

### 3. Visual Test
- Is there a visual that captures the concept?
- Would the visual make sense without text?
- Is it shareable on its own?

### 4. Action Test
- What can someone DO after reading this?
- Is the action clear and immediate?
- Is there a first step they can take today?

### 5. Curiosity Test
- Is there a hook that creates curiosity?
- Why would someone click/read?
- Is there an open loop?

### 6. Value Test
- Would this be valuable even if they never buy anything?
- Are you giving away real insight?
- Is this genuinely useful or just promotional?

## Content Framework

### Framework Creation Template
```
NAME: [Memorable, descriptive name]
PROBLEM: [What challenge does this solve?]
STRUCTURE: [2x2, list, process, equation]
VISUAL: [One image that captures it]
APPLICATION: [How to use it immediately]
```

### The Hook Formula
```
1. Counterintuitive truth: "Most people think X. They're wrong."
2. Curiosity gap: "I spent 100 hours studying Y. Here's what I learned."
3. Specific number: "7 frameworks that changed how I think about Z."
4. Personal story: "I made this mistake. Here's what it taught me."
```

### Content Compound Model
```
Thread (Twitter/X) → Newsletter expansion → LinkedIn post → Blog/SEO → Video
                                              ↓
                                     Podcast appearance
                                              ↓
                                        Speaking/Book
```

### Value Pyramid
```
           ┌─────────┐
           │  Paid   │  Courses, consulting, products
           │ Content │
           ├─────────┤
           │ Premium │  Newsletter, community
           │  Free   │
           ├─────────┤
           │  Free   │  Social media, SEO content
           │ Content │  (This is your marketing)
           └─────────┘
```

**Key**: The free layer should be genuinely valuable. It's not a teaser—it's the product.

### Framework Types That Work
| Type | Structure | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|
| 2x2 Matrix | 4 quadrants | Eisenhower Matrix |
| List | N principles/rules | 7 Habits, 5 Whys |
| Process | Sequential steps | Hero's Journey |
| Equation | X = A + B | E = MC² |
| Spectrum | Continuum/scale | Introvert ↔ Extrovert |
| Pyramid | Hierarchy | Maslow's Hierarchy |
