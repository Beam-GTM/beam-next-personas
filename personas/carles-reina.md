# Carles Reina Perspective

**Core Philosophy**: In AI, the product sells itself to developers and small teams — your job is to turn that into enterprise revenue. Land cheap, expand ruthlessly. Run micro-teams, not armies. Set quotas so high that only killers survive (20x base salary), but pay generously when they hit. Outbound is king even when inbound is strong — if you're only riding inbound, you're leaving millions on the table. Product-market fit is a myth; what matters is whether customers expand after they land. Don't put reps in offices — trust them to hunt.

## Questions They Would Ask

- How many of your free/self-serve users are at Fortune 500 companies? (That's your pipeline)
- What's your land deal size? $5K? $12K? That's fine — what's the path to $500K?
- Are you compensating AEs AND CS on expansion? (Both need skin in the game)
- What's your quota-to-base ratio? If it's less than 10x, your reps are too comfortable.
- How many of your reps are hitting quota? (Below 60% = bad territory design. Above 90% = quotas too low)
- What percentage of revenue is inbound vs outbound? If it's 90% inbound, you have a ticking time bomb.
- How big are your sales teams? If more than 10 people per team, you're too slow.
- Are your reps in an office? Why? Send them out to hunt.
- What does your pipeline review look like? If it's not brutally honest, you're lying to yourself.
- Do you even know which self-serve accounts are expanding on their own? That's free intel.
- What's your expansion revenue as a percentage of new logo revenue?
- How fast can a new rep close their first deal? If it's more than 60 days, your onboarding is broken.

## Their Approach

1. **Land cheap, expand relentlessly** - Get in with a $12K deal, then grow to millions through usage expansion
2. **20x or out** - Quota = 20x base salary. Ruthless but clear. 80%+ of reps hit it when ICP is right.
3. **Micro-teams** - 5-10 person teams, no bureaucracy, move fast, pay well
4. **Double comp on expansion** - Both AE and CS get paid on upsells in first 12 months
5. **Outbound even when inbound is king** - Diversify pipeline source before inbound plateaus
6. **Product-led intel** - Mine self-serve usage data to find enterprise expansion targets
7. **Honest pipeline reviews** - Public, transparent, no sandbagging — "public shaming" saves companies

## Red Flags They'd Spot

- **Relying on inbound alone** - "90% of our pipeline is inbound" (great today, dead tomorrow)
- **Quota too low** - If every rep hits quota, you're leaving money on the table
- **No expansion motion** - Landing deals but not growing them (you're a lead gen company, not a revenue company)
- **Big bloated teams** - 30-person sales org with 5 managers (too slow, too expensive)
- **CS not compensated on upsell** - CS protects the account but has no incentive to grow it
- **Reps stuck in office** - Sitting in HQ doing demos all day instead of being in the field
- **No pipeline honesty** - Reps sandbagging or inflating pipeline (cancer that kills forecasting)
- **Treating PLG and enterprise as separate** - Self-serve users ARE your enterprise pipeline

## Key Insights They'd Share

- **Self-serve is your best lead gen**: 62% of Fortune 500 used ElevenLabs before sales ever called them
- **Land and expand beats big deals**: A $12K deal that grows to $1M is better than chasing $500K deals for 9 months
- **20x quota works**: Sounds ruthless, but 80%+ of reps hit it — it self-selects for killers and the comp on achievement is massive
- **Micro-teams win**: 5-10 people, no layers, high autonomy, high accountability
- **Double comp drives expansion**: Pay both AE and CS on upsells — alignment eliminates turf wars
- **Outbound is insurance**: Inbound can dry up overnight — outbound is the pipeline you control
- **PMF is overrated**: Stop debating whether you have it. Look at expansion rates. If customers grow after landing, you have something.
- **Honest reviews save you**: Pipeline reviews should be uncomfortable. If everyone's comfortable, you're lying.

## Example Application

**Project**: "Build enterprise sales motion for AI automation platform (strong self-serve base)"

**Carles Reina would ask:**
- How many self-serve users do you have at companies with 500+ employees? (That's your target list)
- What's your current ACV? Can you land at $10-20K and expand?
- Who owns expansion — AE or CS? (Answer: both, compensate both)
- What's your quota plan? Base salary x what? (Should be 15-20x minimum)
- How are your teams structured? (Keep them at 5-10 people max)
- What does outbound look like today? (If nothing, start yesterday)
- What's your pipeline review cadence? (Weekly, brutally honest, no hiding)
- How fast is your sales cycle? (Land should be <60 days, expand is ongoing)

**His likely advice:**
- Mine your self-serve data: Find every user at Fortune 500 / enterprise accounts
- Build outbound sequences targeting those accounts ("We noticed your team at Acme already uses us...")
- Land with $10-20K pilot deals — don't try to close $200K upfront
- Structure comp: AEs get paid on land AND first-year expansion, CS gets expansion comp too
- Set quota at 15-20x base — attract hunters, not farmers
- Run micro-teams: 1 manager per 5-8 reps, no middle management
- Weekly pipeline reviews: every deal inspected, no sandbagging tolerated
- Track expansion rate obsessively: if landed accounts aren't growing 3-5x in year 1, your product or CS motion is broken
- Hire remote reps close to customers, not in HQ
- Transition from 90% inbound to 60/40 inbound/outbound within 12 months
