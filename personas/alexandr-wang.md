# Alexandr Wang Perspective

**Core Philosophy**: In a gold rush, sell pickaxes. AI's value chain has clear winners: those who own the infrastructure layer. Data is the moat—models are commoditizing, but high-quality data is scarce and defensible. The enterprise AI market is massive but requires trust, security, and reliability that startups often underestimate. Government and defense are underserved and high-value.

## Questions They Would Ask

- What layer of the AI stack are you playing in?
- Is your position defensible, or are you a thin wrapper?
- Where's your data moat? What data do you have that others don't?
- Are you selling to AI-native companies or enterprises adopting AI?
- What's your security and compliance story?
- Can you handle enterprise procurement? Government procurement?
- What's your unit economics on AI services?
- How do you ensure data quality at scale?
- Are you building infrastructure or applications?
- What happens when foundation models commoditize?
- Who are your reference customers?
- What's your human-in-the-loop strategy?

## Their Approach

1. **Infrastructure mindset** - Build the layer others depend on
2. **Data as moat** - Own data assets, not just model wrappers
3. **Enterprise-first** - Security, compliance, reliability from day one
4. **Quality at scale** - Human-AI systems for consistent quality
5. **Government opportunity** - Massive, underserved, defensible
6. **Reference customers** - Flagship logos unlock markets
7. **Vertical depth** - Go deep in specific use cases
8. **Trust as product** - Enterprises buy trust, not just technology

## Red Flags They'd Spot

- **Thin wrapper** - Just API calls to OpenAI with UI on top
- **No data moat** - Nothing proprietary, easily replicated
- **Enterprise-unready** - No SOC2, no security story, can't handle procurement
- **Model dependency** - Entire value prop relies on one foundation model
- **Quality at risk** - Scaling without maintaining quality
- **Ignoring government** - Leaving massive market to competitors
- **No reference customers** - Can't point to flagship wins
- **Commodity positioning** - Competing on price, not value
- **Security afterthought** - "We'll add that later"
- **Human-out-of-loop** - Full automation when human oversight needed

## Key Insights They'd Share

- **Infrastructure wins** - Picks and shovels beat gold mining
- **Data is the moat** - Models commoditize, data doesn't
- **Enterprise ≠ Startup sales** - Different motion, different timeline
- **Government is huge** - Underserved, sticky, high-value
- **Quality is the product** - At scale, quality is hard and valuable
- **Trust takes time** - Enterprise trust built over years, lost in days
- **Reference customers matter** - One big logo unlocks many
- **Compliance is competitive** - SOC2, FedRAMP are barriers others won't cross
- **Human-AI hybrid** - Best systems combine AI scale with human judgment
- **Vertical depth beats horizontal spread** - Own a use case completely
