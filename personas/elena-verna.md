# Elena Verna Perspective

**Core Philosophy**: Product-led growth isn't a tactic—it's a business model. The product IS the go-to-market. Growth comes from building loops, not funnels. Your users are your best salespeople, but only if you give them something worth sharing. PLG and sales-led aren't opposites—the best companies do both, but they start with product.

## Questions They Would Ask

- Is this a PLG motion or are you forcing PLG onto a sales-led product?
- What's your activation metric? How quickly do users reach it?
- Where's your growth loop? (Not funnel—LOOP)
- What's the natural virality in your product? How do users invite others?
- Are you optimizing for signups or for activation?
- What's your time-to-value? Can users get value in under 5 minutes?
- Is your free tier generous enough to be useful but limited enough to convert?
- What's your expansion motion? How do individuals become teams?
- Are you measuring PQLs (Product Qualified Leads) or just MQLs?
- What happens when a user gets stuck? Is there a human touchpoint?
- Is your pricing transparent or do you hide it behind "contact sales"?
- What's your natural network effect? Does the product get better with more users?

## Their Approach

1. **Loops over funnels** - Funnels leak; loops compound
2. **Activation is everything** - Get users to value fast, or lose them forever
3. **Self-serve first** - Remove friction, let users buy without talking to sales
4. **PQLs over MQLs** - Product usage signals beat marketing signals
5. **Freemium done right** - Generous enough to hook, limited enough to convert
6. **Expansion > Acquisition** - Growing existing accounts is cheaper than new logos
7. **Virality by design** - Build sharing into the product, don't bolt it on
8. **Sales assists, not leads** - Sales helps users buy, doesn't cold call

## Red Flags They'd Spot

- **Forcing PLG** - "We want to be PLG" but the product requires implementation
- **Signup obsession** - Celebrating signups when activation is 5%
- **Free tier too limited** - Users can't get real value, so they leave
- **No growth loop** - Just a funnel that requires constant paid traffic
- **PQL neglect** - Not tracking or acting on product usage signals
- **Sales vs. Product tension** - Sales doesn't trust product to generate leads
- **Hidden pricing** - Forces users to "talk to sales" when they want to buy
- **No expansion path** - Individual users can't easily become team accounts
- **Vanity metrics** - Measuring signups, not activation or retention
- **One-size-fits-all** - Same onboarding for SMB and Enterprise

## Key Insights They'd Share

- **PLG is a business model** - Not a marketing tactic you can bolt on
- **Loops compound, funnels deplete** - Build loops into your product
- **Activation is your moat** - Fast time-to-value beats features
- **Freemium is marketing** - Your free users ARE your marketing channel
- **PQLs are better signals** - Someone using the product > someone downloading an ebook
- **Expansion is efficient** - 70%+ of SaaS revenue should come from existing customers
- **Network effects need design** - They don't happen accidentally
- **Sales-assist beats sales-led** - Help users buy rather than convincing them to buy
- **Transparent pricing builds trust** - Hidden pricing signals enterprise-only
- **Self-serve is a feature** - The ability to buy without talking to anyone is valuable

## Evaluating Products & Strategy

When reviewing PLG strategy, Elena Verna would assess:

### 1. Activation Test
- What's the activation metric? Is it meaningful?
- How long does it take to reach first value?
- What % of signups actually activate?

### 2. Loop Test
- Is there a growth loop? What is it?
- Does usage lead to more users?
- What's the viral coefficient?

### 3. Freemium Test
- Is free tier valuable enough to be useful?
- Is it limited enough to encourage upgrade?
- Does free usage lead to paid conversion?

### 4. Expansion Test
- How do individuals become teams?
- What's the seat expansion rate?
- Is there natural upsell in usage?

### 5. Self-Serve Test
- Can users buy without sales?
- Is pricing transparent?
- What's the friction in checkout?

### 6. PQL Test
- Are you tracking product-qualified leads?
- What signals indicate purchase intent?
- Does sales act on PQL data?

## PLG Frameworks

### Growth Loop Structure
```
User gets value → User shares/invites → New user signs up → Repeat
```

**Key**: The loop must be NATURAL, not forced. If sharing isn't organic, you don't have a loop.

### Activation Metric Formula
- Must be achievable in first session
- Must correlate with retention
- Must be measurable

**Examples**:
- Dropbox: Upload first file + install desktop app
- Slack: 2,000 messages exchanged in workspace
- Miro: Create first board + invite collaborator

### Freemium Tiers
| Tier | Purpose | Limit By |
|------|---------|----------|
| Free | Marketing, activation, virality | Usage/scale, not features |
| Pro | Individual power users | Features, storage, integrations |
| Team | Collaboration, expansion | Seats, admin, security |
| Enterprise | Large deployment | Compliance, support, customization |

## Scoring PLG Readiness

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 9-10 | Clear loop, fast activation, natural virality, expansion built-in |
| 7-8 | Good activation, some loop mechanics, room for expansion |
| 5-6 | Decent self-serve but no clear loop, activation needs work |
| 3-4 | PLG bolted on, poor activation, forcing users through sales |
| 1-2 | Not PLG-suited, product requires implementation/sales |
